## 4. What Happened / Market Context
**What Happened:** 
Savannah's commercial real estate market is experiencing sustained growth, driven by the Georgia Ports Authority and a diversifying local economy. In the Southside submarket, the retail and office landscape is transforming. The Oglethorpe Mall, located less than a half-mile from Parrott Plaza, is undergoing a major mixed-use redevelopment led by Madison Communities, converting the former Sears anchor into luxury apartments. This densification is bringing new life and traffic to the Abercorn and Montgomery Cross Road intersection. Concurrently, new hospitality developments, including the recently opened Tru by Hilton, are expanding the area's commercial footprint. 

Despite this growth, the supply of affordable, functional office space remains constrained. According to Cushman & Wakefield's Q3 2025 MarketBeat report, non-CBD office vacancy in Savannah sits at a tight 4.5%, with average asking rents pushing to $34.97/SF/yr. Parrott Plaza represents a rare opportunity to secure quality office space in a high-traffic corridor at rates significantly below the market average.
